## The Seat Structure (Yes, It's Confusing)

Figma now uses four seat types:

- **Full seats** ($12-90/month): Complete access to Figma Design, Dev Mode, Slides, and FigJam
- **Dev seats**: For developers who need to inspect designs and export code
- **Collab seats**: Access to FigJam and Figma Slides for brainstorming and presenting
- **View seats**: Free for everyoneâ€”they can view and comment but not edit

The key insight? Don't pay for Full seats for people who just need to look at designs or leave comments. Use the free View seats liberally.

## Quick FAQ

**Q: Can I use Figma offline?**
A: There's a desktop app that offers limited offline functionality, but Figma is primarily cloud-based and works best with an internet connection.

**Q: How does Figma compare to Adobe XD or Sketch?**
A: Figma's browser-based approach and real-time collaboration put it ahead for team workflows. Sketch is Mac-only and less collaborative. Adobe XD has been deprioritized by Adobe.

**Q: Do I need coding skills to use Figma?**
A: Nope. Figma is designed for designers. However, understanding basic front-end concepts helps when creating prototypes or working with developers.

**Q: What happens to my files if I cancel?**
A: You can still view and export your files, but you won't be able to edit them. It's worth exporting important work before downgrading.

**Q: Is there a student discount?**
A: Yes! Students and educators get the full Professional plan completely free through Figma's education program. You'll need to verify your status.

**Q: Can Figma replace all my design tools?**
A: For digital product design, pretty much yes. For print design, photo editing, or illustration, you'll still want specialized tools. Figma doesn't have CMYK support or 300 DPI export options.
